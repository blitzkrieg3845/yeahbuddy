Calories burned per minute calculation is a method used to calculate how many calories a person burns during a certain activity. This calculation depends on the intensity of the activity, the weight of the person, and the duration of the activity.
The amount of calories burned in a minute can be calculated using the formula MET (Metabolic Equivalent) of activity and calorie consumption per kilogram (MET x kg x 3.5 / 200). Here, 1 MET is equal to the amount of calories a person consumes at rest (approximately 3.5 ml O2/kg/min).
For example, to calculate the approximate number of calories a 70-kilogram person would burn if they walked for 30 minutes, we can follow these steps:
Find the MET of the walk. The MET value of the walk is approximately 3.5 METs.
Calculate the person's calorie consumption per kilogram. This is calculated by multiplying the person's weight (70 kg) by 3.5 (ml O2/kg/min) and dividing by 200. In this example, the person's calorie consumption per kilogram is approximately 24 kcal/minute.
Calculate calories burned per minute. This is 3.5 MET x 70 kg x 3.5 ml O2/kg/minute / 200 = approximately 12.25 kcal/minute.
Calculate the total calories burned. This is to multiply the calories burned per minute (12.25 kcal/minute) by 30 minutes (about 368 calories).
As a result, it can be calculated that a 70 kilogram person would burn about 368 calories if he walked for 30 minutes. This calculation can be done similarly for other activities.
